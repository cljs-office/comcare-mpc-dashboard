1 · Austrade — OAIC privacy complaint

Forum: agency first → OAIC
Basis
Interference with privacy under the Privacy Act 1988 — an APP breach by Austrade in how it collected, used, disclosed or secured your personal (and sensitive medical) information through the posting cessation, Comcare and leave processes.
Why strong
Austrade is a Commonwealth agency, so the private-sector employee-records exemption (s7B(3)) does not apply — agencies must comply with the APPs for employee information. Health/medical data is sensitive information with higher protection.
Confirm
The specific incident(s): what information, disclosed to whom, when, and the harm. Needs dates + documents to anchor each alleged breach.
Limitation
No fixed bar, but OAIC may decline a complaint about conduct more than 12 months old (s41 discretion). Act within ~12 months of each incident.
Cost
Free to lodge. Remedies: apology, correction, policy change, and compensation for loss/distress.
Next action
List each privacy incident with date + evidence, then complain to Austrade's privacy officer first; if no adequate response in 30 days, lodge with OAIC.

3 · Fair Work — general protections / adverse action

Forum: FWC s372 → Federal Court / FCFCOA
Basis
Adverse action taken because you exercised a workplace right — e.g. raising concerns about the manager's conduct, taking certified leave, and lodging the Comcare claim (FW Act ss 340–341). Candidate adverse actions: ceasing the posting early, pressing relocation during certified incapacity, the call during leave that triggered a panic attack.
Time limit
You were not dismissed, so the 21-day limit does not apply (that is dismissal-only, s366). Non-dismissal general protections: apply to the FWC under s372 while employed; civil remedy carries a ~6-year limitation, then Federal Court or FCFCOA if unresolved.
Why strong
Reverse onus: once you show adverse action + a workplace right, Austrade must prove the action was not for the prohibited reason.
Risk
Must evidence the causal link (right → action). Narrative-consistency risk with Comcare/MPC: arguments here must not hand Austrade the "reasonable administrative action" point. Sooner is better for evidence and an ongoing relationship.
Next action
Map each adverse act to a workplace right and date; decide s372 conciliation (FWC) vs court, and align wording with the MPC and Comcare cases.
